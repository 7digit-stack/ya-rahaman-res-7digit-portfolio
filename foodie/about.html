Every Biriyani Has a Story

Ours starts with one handi on Mambakkam Main Road — and a recipe we still refuse to rush.

One Family Recipe Two Decades on the Dum

Ya Rahman began in 2003 as a small family kitchen in Medavakkam. The recipe came first — a Hyderabadi-style dum biriyani perfected at home long before it ever had a signboard. The first customers were neighbours. Then their neighbours. Then, somehow, most of Chennai.

Twenty-three years on, the method hasn't changed. Aged basmati, meat that arrives fresh every morning, whole spices ground in our own kitchen, and a handi sealed with dough and left alone on the dum until it's ready. If a batch isn't right, it doesn't leave the kitchen — simple as that.

What drives us

Our Values

Three principles guide everything we do — from sourcing ingredients to serving your table.

Quality First

The rice is aged, the meat comes in fresh every single morning, and the spices are ground right here. If we wouldn't serve it at our own table, it doesn't go in the handi.

Authentic Dum Cooking

Layered by hand, sealed with dough, cooked low and slow. Yes, it takes longer. Some things shouldn't be optimised.

Community & Care

We've fed three generations of some families — school treats, iftar evenings, wedding buckets. Being part of the neighbourhood is the whole point.

How We Got Here

From One Handi to Fifteen Kitchens

2003

One kitchen in Medavakkam

Ya Rahman opens with a single dum handi and a family recipe. No marketing, no menu card — word spreads one plate at a time.

2011

The GST Road years

Pallavaram first, then Tambaram and Chrompet. Regulars from the airport corridor start timing their journeys around lunch.

2018

Following the techies down OMR

Egattur to Siruseri — biriyani lands on the IT corridor, and Friday office lunches are never quite the same again.

Today

15 kitchens, one standard

Every branch still cooks the same way: layered by hand, sealed with dough, and opened only when it's ready.
